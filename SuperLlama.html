Guard Llamas

Using llamas as livestock guards in North America began in the early 1980s, and some sheep producers have used llamas successfully since then. They are used most commonly in the western regions of the United States, where larger predators, such as coyotes and feral dogs, are prevalent. Typically, a single male Llama is used as a guard. Using llamas as guards has reduced the losses to predators for many producers.
